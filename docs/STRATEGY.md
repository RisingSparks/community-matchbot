# Strategy: Leveraging Theme Camps + Art Projects for Intergenerational Collaboration

## The Challenge: Demographic Continuity
Burning Man’s demographic challenge isn’t just about attendance—it’s about continuity. 
- **First-timers:** ~30% of participants.
- **Under-30s:** Fallen from 30% (2014) to 12% (today).
- **Risk:** The ecosystem risks aging faster than it evolves without new energy and cultural handoff.

## The Solution: Theme Camps + Art Projects as Gateway
The opportunity lies in strengthening theme camps and art projects as the natural connective tissue of Black Rock City. They are where culture, logistics, and social learning intersect.

### Why Camps + Art Projects Are the Leverage Point
1. **Self-Interested Organizations:** Camps and art projects need new blood to survive; new energy is essential for longevity and preventing lead burnout.
2. **Infrastructure/Lower Barriers:** Camps provide shared resources (power, water, shade, food), and art teams provide concrete build pathways, both lowering barriers to contribution.
3. **Acculturation Incubators:** They provide "high-trust, high-accountability micro-communities" that facilitate social learning and principle absorption.

## Goal: Mentorship & Onboarding
We need to move beyond simple recruitment to active builder **mentorship**. Camps and art teams should serve as classrooms where experienced burners onboard newcomers, passing on the tacit knowledge that keeps Burning Man alive across generations.

## Positioning Clarification
This is not a recruitment tool. It is a discovery tool.

- It does not "feed camps" or prop up unsustainable scale.
- It does not promise placement or force camp/project growth.
- It helps self-motivated people find aligned communities and build pathways.

If a camp or project has outgrown what its own community can sustain, getting
smaller is a healthy outcome. The goal here is better discovery and alignment,
not preserving every organization at any cost.

## Why This Matters Post-Pandemic
Theme camps and art projects remain key community entry points because they can
still provide:

1. **Affordability:** Shared infrastructure lowers entry barriers for people
without RVs or large budgets.
2. **Acculturation:** High-accountability communities teach participation
norms, work culture, and stewardship.
3. **Social Network Access:** Camps and art teams are still strong mentorship
nodes as friends-of-friends pipelines have weakened.
