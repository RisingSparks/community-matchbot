# Strategy: Leveraging Theme Camps for Intergenerational Collaboration

## The Challenge: Demographic Continuity
Burning Man’s demographic challenge isn’t just about attendance—it’s about continuity. 
- **First-timers:** ~30% of participants.
- **Under-30s:** Fallen from 30% (2014) to 12% (today).
- **Risk:** The ecosystem risks aging faster than it evolves without new energy and cultural handoff.

## The Solution: Theme Camps as Gateway
The opportunity lies in strengthening theme camps as the natural connective tissue of Black Rock City. They are where culture, logistics, and social learning intersect.

### Why Camps Are the Leverage Point
1. **Self-Interested Organizations:** Camps need new blood to survive; new energy is essential for longevity and preventing lead burnout.
2. **Infrastructure/Lower Barriers:** Camps provide shared resources (power, water, shade, food), lowering logistical and financial barriers to entry.
3. **Acculturation Incubators:** They provide a "high-trust, high-accountability micro-community" that facilitates social learning and principle absorption.

## Goal: Mentorship & Onboarding
We need to move beyond simple recruitment to active builder **mentorship**. Camps should serve as classrooms where experienced burners onboard newcomers, passing on the tacit knowledge that keeps Burning Man alive across generations.
